# STAR Interview Stories

> 📖 **Instructions:** The STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) is the standard format for behavioral interview answers. Prepare 6 stories from your GRC project that you can adapt to common interview questions.
>
> **One worked example** is provided. Write 5 more based on your actual project experience.

---

## How to Use STAR

| Letter | Meaning | What to Say | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| **S** | Situation | Set the scene — what was the context? | 15 seconds |
| **T** | Task | What was your specific responsibility? | 15 seconds |
| **A** | Action | What did you actually do? (Be specific) | 60 seconds |
| **R** | Result | What was the outcome? What did you learn? | 30 seconds |

**Total: ~2 minutes per answer.** Practice out loud until it feels natural.

## Story 1: Building a GRC Program from Scratch (Worked Example)

**Best for questions like:** "Tell me about a time you built something from scratch" / "Describe a complex project you led" / "How do you approach ambiguous problems?"

**Situation:** I was learning GRC and needed to demonstrate that I could build a complete governance, risk, and compliance program — not just understand the theory, but actually produce the deliverables a GRC professional would create in their first 90 days at a new company.

**Task:** My goal was to build a full GRC program for a fictional 100-person cybersecurity startup, covering security policies, risk management, and compliance across three major frameworks — NIST CSF 2.0, SOC 2, and ISO 27001.

**Action:** I started with governance — researching industry-standard policy structures and writing 5 security policies with operational procedures. Then I built a risk management program, identifying over 15 risks, scoring each with a 5×5 matrix, and creating treatment plans. For compliance, I documented controls across all three frameworks and built a cross-framework mapping showing where they overlap. Finally, I learned Python to automate the workflows — building tools that score risks, look up equivalent controls across frameworks, and generate executive reports.

**Result:** I produced a complete, portfolio-ready GRC program hosted on GitHub. The cross-framework mapping was the most valuable deliverable — it showed that approximately 70% of controls overlap across frameworks, meaning a company can implement once and satisfy multiple standards. The project gave me concrete examples for every common GRC interview question and demonstrated both conceptual understanding and technical capability.

## Practice Tips

1. **Say them out loud** — Reading silently is not practice. Speak them aloud until they flow naturally.
2. **Time yourself** — Each story should be ~2 minutes. If it's over 3 minutes, trim it.
3. **Adapt, don't memorize** — Know the key points, but adjust the emphasis based on what the interviewer is asking.
4. **Quantify when possible** — "15+ risks" is better than "many risks." "3 frameworks" is better than "multiple frameworks."
5. **End strong** — The Result should include what you learned or how it's applicable to the role you're interviewing for.
