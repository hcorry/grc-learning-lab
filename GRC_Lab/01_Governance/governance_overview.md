## 3. How Policies Relate to Each Other

<!-- YOUR WORK: Explain the relationships between your policies. This shows an auditor you understand the big picture.

Think about these connections:
- The **Information Security Policy** is the "parent" — all other policies derive from it
- The **Data Classification Policy** feeds into the **Access Control Policy** (you can't control access appropriately without knowing data sensitivity)
- The **Access Control Policy** feeds into the **Acceptable Use Policy** (access rules determine what "acceptable use" means)
- The **Incident Response Policy** is triggered when any other policy is violated
- All policies reference each other in their Policy Statements sections

Draw this out as a text diagram or describe the relationships in 3-4 paragraphs.

Example text diagram:
```
Information Security Policy (top-level)
├── Data Classification Policy
│   └── informs → Access Control Policy
├── Access Control Policy
│   └── referenced by → Acceptable Use Policy
├── Acceptable Use Policy
│   └── violations trigger → Incident Response Policy
└── Incident Response Policy
    └── covers violations of all policies above
```
-->
