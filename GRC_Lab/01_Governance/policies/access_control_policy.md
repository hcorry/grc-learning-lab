## 3. Roles & Responsibilities

> **✏️ YOUR WORK**
> YOUR WORK: Define roles. Access control involves more people than most policies.
>
> Key roles to consider:
> - **System/Resource Owners** — approve access requests for their systems
> - **IT/Security Team** — provision and deprovision access, manage identity systems
> - **Managers** — request access for their team members, approve changes
> - **HR** — trigger onboarding/offboarding access workflows
> - **Employees** — protect their credentials, report unauthorized access
> - **CISO** — owns the policy, oversees access reviews
>
> Important: Access control is where "separation of duties" matters — the person requesting access shouldn't be the same person approving it.

## 4. Policy Statements

> **✏️ YOUR WORK**
> YOUR WORK: Write the access control rules. This policy typically has more subsections than others because access control touches everything.
>
> Structure suggestion:
>
> ### 4.1 Access Provisioning
> (How access is granted — formal request, manager approval, owner approval, etc.)
>
> ### 4.2 Least Privilege
> (Users get only the minimum access needed for their role. No "just in case" access.)
>
> ### 4.3 Authentication
> (Password requirements, MFA requirements, session timeouts. Which systems require MFA?)
>
> ### 4.4 Privileged Access
> (Admin/root accounts — how are they managed? Who can have them? Are they monitored?)
>
> ### 4.5 Access Reviews
> (How often is access reviewed? Who conducts reviews? What happens to unused access?)
>
> ### 4.6 Offboarding / Access Revocation
> (What happens when someone leaves? How fast must access be removed? Checklist of systems.)
>
> ### 4.7 Remote Access
> (VPN requirements, MFA for remote access, approved methods of remote connection.)
>
> ### 4.8 Service Accounts
> (Non-human accounts used by applications — who owns them? How are credentials rotated?)
>
> Key concepts to weave in:
> - **Least privilege** — only the access you need
> - **Separation of duties** — no single person controls an entire critical process
> - **Need to know** — access to data based on job requirements
> - **Defense in depth** — multiple layers of access control
