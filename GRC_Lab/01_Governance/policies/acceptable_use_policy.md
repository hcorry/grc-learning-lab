## 4. Policy Statements

> **✏️ YOUR WORK**
> YOUR WORK: This is the core of the policy. Write specific rules about acceptable and unacceptable use of company systems.
>
> Structure suggestion — organize by topic:
>
> ### 4.1 General Use
> (Rules about overall system use — company systems are for business purposes, limited personal use may be acceptable, etc.)
>
> ### 4.2 Email and Communications
> (Rules about email usage — no forwarding company data to personal email, be professional, watch for phishing, etc.)
>
> ### 4.3 Internet Use
> (What's acceptable — research, cloud tools. What's prohibited — malicious sites, bypassing security controls, excessive personal use.)
>
> ### 4.4 Software and Downloads
> (Only approved software, no unauthorized installations, no pirated software, etc.)
>
> ### 4.5 Data Handling
> (Don't store company data on personal devices/cloud accounts, follow the Data Classification Policy, etc.)
>
> ### 4.6 Remote Work
> (Secure your home network, lock your screen, don't use public WiFi without VPN, etc.)
>
> ### 4.7 Personal Devices (BYOD)
> (If personal devices are allowed for work — what rules apply? If not allowed — say so.)
>
> For each subsection, write 3-5 "must" or "must not" statements.
>
> Common mistakes to avoid:
> - Being too vague ("use systems responsibly" — what does that mean?)
> - Being too restrictive ("no personal use ever" — unrealistic for a startup)
> - Forgetting remote work — most startups have remote workers
