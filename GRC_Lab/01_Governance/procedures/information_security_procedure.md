# Information Security Procedure

**Companion to:** Information Security Policy
**Document Owner:** CISO
**Version:** 1.0

> ℹ️ **This is a worked example.** This shows what a companion procedure looks like. A procedure takes the policy's high-level rules and turns them into step-by-step operational instructions.

---

## 1. Security Awareness Training Procedure

**Policy reference:** Section 4.4 — Security Awareness

### New Employee Onboarding

1. HR sends security training invitation within 1 business day of start date
2. Employee completes online security awareness training module (covers: phishing recognition, password hygiene, data handling, incident reporting)
3. Employee takes the post-training assessment (minimum passing score: 80%)
4. Employee signs the security policy acknowledgment form
5. IT verifies training completion before granting access to production systems
6. HR files the signed acknowledgment in the employee's personnel record

### Annual Refresher

1. CISO sends annual training reminder to all employees in Q1
2. Employees complete refresher training within 30 days of notification
3. Employees re-sign the policy acknowledgment
4. CISO follows up with non-completions at 15 and 25 days
5. After 30 days, non-completions are escalated to department heads

---

## 2. Security Policy Review Procedure

**Policy reference:** Section 6 — Review Schedule

### Annual Review Process

1. CISO schedules annual policy review in Q4
2. CISO reviews each policy for:
   - Relevance to current business operations
   - Alignment with current compliance requirements
   - Gaps identified through incidents or audits during the year
3. CISO drafts updates and circulates to department heads for feedback (2-week review period)
4. CISO incorporates feedback and submits updated policies to executive team for approval
5. Approved policies are published and communicated to all employees
6. Version history is updated in each policy document

---

## 3. Third-Party Security Assessment Procedure

**Policy reference:** Section 4.5 — Third-Party Security

### Before Granting Vendor Access

1. Business owner submits vendor security assessment request to CISO
2. CISO sends security questionnaire to vendor (covers: encryption, access controls, incident response, data handling, certifications)
3. Vendor returns completed questionnaire within 10 business days
4. CISO reviews responses and scores vendor risk level:
   - **Low risk** — vendor has SOC 2 or ISO 27001 certification, satisfactory questionnaire responses
   - **Medium risk** — missing certification but satisfactory controls described
   - **High risk** — significant control gaps identified
5. Low/Medium risk: CISO approves with conditions documented in vendor agreement
6. High risk: CISO meets with vendor to discuss remediation, or recommends finding an alternative vendor
7. Approved vendors are added to the vendor register with next review date (annually)

---

> 📝 **Notice the pattern:** Each procedure has numbered steps that someone could follow without needing to make judgment calls. The policy says "vendors must undergo a security assessment" — the procedure says exactly HOW that assessment works, step by step.
