# Day 2 Guide: Writing Procedures & the Governance Overview

**Estimated time: 6–7 hours**

## What You're Building

Today you'll write a **companion procedure for each of your 5 policies**, then complete the **Governance Overview** that ties the whole framework together.

If policies are the "what and why," procedures are the "how." Every policy needs a procedure — otherwise employees know what the rule is but not how to follow it.

By the end of today, you'll have a complete governance framework: 5 policies + 5 procedures + a governance overview.

---

## Policy vs. Procedure: The Key Distinction

This is a common interview question: **"What's the difference between a policy and a procedure?"**

| | Policy | Procedure |
|---|---|---|
| **Level** | Strategic — the "what" and "why" | Operational — the "how" |
| **Audience** | Everyone in scope | People performing specific tasks |
| **Changes** | Rarely (annual review) | More often (as processes evolve) |
| **Length** | 1-3 pages | Can be longer — step-by-step detail |
| **Example** | "All incidents must be reported within 1 hour" | "Step 1: Open the incident tracking system. Step 2: Click 'New Incident'..." |

### Interview Tip

When asked this question, always give a concrete example of the same topic at both levels — like the incident response example above. It shows you understand the distinction in practice, not just in theory.

## Your Four Procedures to Write

Write a companion procedure for each policy you completed on Day 1.

1. **Acceptable Use Procedure** — How employees request approved tools, report misuse, and handle personal device use in practice
2. **Access Control Procedure** — Step-by-step for onboarding access, access reviews, and offboarding (revoking access when someone leaves)
3. **Incident Response Procedure** — The exact steps to take when an incident is detected: who to call, what to document, how to escalate
4. **Data Classification Procedure** — How to classify a new document, label data in each system, and handle each classification level day-to-day

---

## The Governance Overview

After your procedures, complete the **Governance Overview** (in the sidebar). This is the document an auditor or new executive would read first to understand SecureTech's entire security governance program.

It's completed last because it summarizes everything you've already built:
- How all 5 policies relate to each other
- Who owns what across the organization
- Your annual governance calendar
- How the framework improves over time

This is a strong portfolio piece — it shows you can think about governance holistically, not just document-by-document.

## When You're Done with Governance

You should be able to:
1. Explain what each policy covers and why it's needed
2. Walk through the difference between a policy and its companion procedure
3. Describe how all 5 policies connect to each other (the governance framework)
4. Talk through your process for creating a governance program at a new organization

Practice explaining these out loud — governance is almost always in the first 10 minutes of a GRC interview.
